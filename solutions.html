How does air pollution affect your health?


Numerous studies over the years have repeatedly shown that increased outdoor air levels of fine particulate matter correspond to increased hospitalizations for heart disease,stroke, diabetes, pneumonia, chronic obstructive pulmonary disease exacerbation, and other serious health problems. Both long-term exposure and short-term exposure seem to matter.

A study published this year looked at global models of pollution levels and risk assessments of the world population over 14 years. It ties fossil fuel combustion alone to nearly nine million premature deaths worldwide in 2018 — that's one in five deaths — including more than 350,000 in the US. Most of these deaths are due to heart attacks and strokes.

Who is especially vulnerable to the potential effects of air pollution? Anyone who is elderly, young, or pregnant, and anyone with underlying diseases such as a heart or a lung condition. Additionally, people living in low-income communities, which are often situated near industrial plants or high-traffic areas, are disproportionately affected.



Basic norms every government should follow


Renewable Energy Deployment


Governments and organizations can promote the widespread deployment of renewable energy sources such as solar, wind, and hydroelectric power. This involves investing in renewable energy infrastructure, providing financial incentives for renewable energy projects, and streamlining regulatory processes for their development.


Incentives for Clean Energy Technologies

Governments can offer financial incentives, subsidies, tax breaks, and feed-in tariffs to encourage the adoption of clean energy technologies by businesses and individuals. These incentives can make renewable energy more economically competitive with fossil fuels, accelerating the transition to cleaner energy sources.



Research and Development

Investing in research and development (R&D) efforts aimed at improving the efficiency and cost-effectiveness of renewable energy technologies is crucial. By funding R&D initiatives, governments can support innovation in areas such as energy storage, grid integration, and renewable energy conversion technologies.




Net-Zero Targets


Setting ambitious targets for achieving net-zero carbon emissions
can drive investment and innovation in clean energy solutions.
Governments can implement policies and regulations that mandate
a transition to renewable energy sources while phasing out the
use of fossil fuels in power generation and other sectors.


Education and Outreach

Educating the public about the benefits of clean energy and the importance of transitioning away from fossil fuels is crucial. Outreach efforts can raise awareness, change attitudes, and mobilize support for clean energy policies and initiatives at the local, national, and global levels.




What can You do ?


Conserve Energy - at Home, at Work, Everywhere


Encouraging individuals to be mindful of their energy usage and to implement energy-saving practices in all aspects of life. This can include turning off lights and electronics when not in use, using energy-efficient appliances, and optimizing heating, ventilation, and air conditioning (HVAC) systems for efficiency.


Look for the ENERGY STAR Label

Recommending the use of ENERGY STAR certified products for home and office equipment. ENERGY STAR labels indicate that products meet strict energy efficiency guidelines set by the Environmental Protection Agency (EPA), helping consumers choose energy-efficient options that can save money and reduce emissions.



Public Transport


Adopting public transportation as a preferred mode of travel contributes significantly to reducing air pollution, enhancing air quality, and creating healthier and more sustainable communities. By investing in and prioritizing public transit infrastructure and services, cities can achieve cleaner air, improved public health, and a more environmentally responsible transportation system.

Promoting alternative transportation methods to reduce reliance on single-occupancy vehicles and minimize emissions from transportation. Carpooling, using public transit, biking, or walking can help decrease traffic congestion, air pollution, and greenhouse gas emissions.


Use Environmentally Safe Products

Encouraging the use of environmentally safe paints and cleaning products to reduce exposure to harmful chemicals and minimize air and water pollution. Choosing eco-friendly alternatives can protect human health and the environment.



Consider Using Gas Logs Instead of Wood

Suggesting the use of gas logs as an alternative to burning wood for heating purposes. Gas logs produce fewer emissions, particulate matter, and indoor air pollutants compared to traditional wood-burning fireplaces, contributing to better air quality and respiratory health.



Mulch or Compost Yard Waste


Recommending the practice of mulching or composting leaves and yard waste instead of burning or sending them to landfills. Mulching and composting recycle organic matter, enrich soil health, and reduce greenhouse gas emissions associated with waste disposal.
