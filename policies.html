National Clean Air Programme (NCAP):



Overview
National Clean Air Programme has been launched by the Ministry of Environment, Forest and Climate Change as a comprehensive initiative in partnership with various Ministries and States to improve air quality at city, regional and national level.



Goal
Meet prescribed annual average ambient air quality standards at all locations in the country in a stipulated timeframe (long-term).

Target
Tentative national level target of 20%–30% reduction of Particulate Matter (PM10 and PM2.5) concentration by 2024 is proposed under NCAP.



Objectives
To ensure stringent implementation of mitigation measures for prevention, control and abatement of air pollution.
To augment and evolve effective and proficient ambient air quality monitoring network across the country for ensuring a comprehensive and reliable database.
To augment public awareness and capacity-building measures encompassing data dissemination and public outreach programmes for inclusive public participation and for ensuring trained manpower and infrastructure on air pollution.

Approach
Collaborative, multi-scale and cross-sectoral coordination between the relevant central ministries, state governments and local bodies.
Use the smart cities framework to launch the NCAP in the 47 smart cities falling in the list of the 131 non-attainment cities
The NCAP will be dynamic and will continue to evolve based on the additional available scientific and technical information as they emerge and in response to international best practices and experiences that are available.

Status of Air Quality
Out of 131 identified cities, decrease in PM10 Concentration has been observed in 88 cities during 2022-23 as compared to levels during FY 2017-18.




Pradhan Mantri Ujjwala Yojana (PMUY)






Overview
Pradhan Mantri Ujjwala Yojana (PMUY) is a flagship social welfare scheme launched by the Government of India in May 2016. The scheme aims to provide free LPG (liquefied petroleum gas) connections to below-poverty-line households across the country.

Goal
The primary goal of PMUY is to promote clean cooking fuel adoption among economically disadvantaged households, thereby reducing the usage of traditional cooking fuels such as biomass and kerosene. By providing access to clean LPG, the scheme aims to improve health outcomes, particularly among women and children, and mitigate indoor air pollution.



Target
The scheme targets below-poverty-line households identified through the Socio-Economic Caste Census (SECC) data. The target under the scheme was to release 8 Crore LPG Connections to the deprived households by March 2020.

Objectives
To alleviate the economic burden of purchasing cooking gas cylinders for poor households.
To reduce indoor air pollution caused by the use of traditional cooking fuels, which has adverse health impacts, especially on women and children.
To empower women by enhancing their safety, health, and productivity through the adoption of clean cooking fuels.
To promote sustainable development and environmental conservation by reducing deforestation and greenhouse gas emissions associated with traditional cooking practices.

Approach
PMUY adopts a demand-driven approach where eligible beneficiaries can apply for free LPG connections through authorized distributors. The scheme provides financial assistance to cover the cost of LPG connections, which includes a refundable security deposit for the cylinder and pressure regulator, administrative charges, and cost of the first refill.

Progress
On 7th September 2019, Hon’ble Prime Minister of India handed over the 8th Crore LPG connection in Aurangabad, Maharashtra.
Ujjwala 2.0: Additional allocation of 1.6 Crore LPG Connections under PMUY scheme with special facility to migrant households. Target number of connections under Ujjwala 2.0 was achieved during Dec’22, thus taking overall connections under the scheme to 9.6 Crore.
Government of India has approved release of additional 75 lakh connections under PMUY Scheme, taking overall target under the scheme to 10.35 Crore, against which connections are getting released now.




Result
Pradhan Mantri Ujjwala Yojana (PMUY) has significantly reduced indoor air pollution by promoting the adoption of clean LPG as a cooking fuel.The implementation of PMUY has contributed to environmental conservation by curbing deforestation and mitigating greenhouse gas emissions.




Bharat Stage (BS) VI emission standards






Overview
Bharat Stage (BS) VI emission standards, implemented in April 2020, regulate vehicular emissions in India. These standards mandate the use of cleaner fuels and stricter emission norms to reduce air pollution from vehicles.

Goal
The primary goal of BS VI emission standards is to improve air quality by significantly reducing vehicular emissions of harmful pollutants such as nitrogen oxides (NOx), particulate matter (PM), and hydrocarbons. The standards aim to bring Indian vehicular emissions in line with global environmental norms.

Target
The target of BS VI emission standards is to ensure that all vehicles sold and registered in India comply with the prescribed emission norms. This includes both two-wheelers and four-wheelers, as well as commercial vehicles, ensuring a comprehensive reduction in vehicular emissions across the country.



Objectives
To reduce the emission of harmful pollutants from vehicles, including nitrogen oxides, particulate matter, and hydrocarbons.
To align Indian vehicular emission standards with global benchmarks, ensuring environmental compatibility and sustainability.
To improve air quality and public health by curbing vehicular emissions, which are a significant contributor to air pollution in urban areas.
To promote the adoption of cleaner technologies and fuels in the automotive sector, fostering innovation and sustainable transportation solutions.


Approach
The approach to implementing BS VI emission standards involves collaboration between the government, automotive industry, and regulatory bodies. It includes mandating the use of cleaner fuels such as BS VI-compliant petrol and diesel, as well as enforcing stricter emission norms for vehicle manufacturers. Additionally, the government provides support and incentives to facilitate the transition to BS VI-compliant vehicles and technologies.

Conclusion
BS VI emission standards represent a significant step towards reducing vehicular emissions and improving air quality in India. By mandating the use of cleaner fuels and stricter emission norms, these standards aim to mitigate the adverse effects of vehicular pollution on public health and the environment. However, effective enforcement, continued innovation, and public awareness are essential to achieving long-term sustainability and ensuring the success of BS VI emission standards.




Electric Mobility Mission (FAME India scheme)






Overview
The Electric Mobility Mission, launched in April 2015 as part of the Faster Adoption and Manufacturing of (Hybrid &) Electric Vehicles in India (FAME India) scheme, aims to promote the adoption of electric vehicles (EVs) and reduce vehicular emissions. It focuses on accelerating the deployment of electric and hybrid vehicles across the country, fostering sustainable transportation solutions, and reducing dependence on fossil fuels.

Goal
The primary goal of the Electric Mobility Mission is to facilitate the widespread adoption of electric vehicles in India. By incentivizing the production and purchase of EVs, the mission aims to reduce vehicular emissions, improve air quality, and mitigate the environmental impact of transportation. Additionally, it seeks to enhance energy security and promote the growth of the domestic electric vehicle industry.

Target
The target of the Electric Mobility Mission is to increase the penetration of electric and hybrid vehicles in the Indian automotive market. This includes setting targets for the adoption of electric vehicles across different vehicle segments, such as two-wheelers, three-wheelers, four-wheelers, and commercial vehicles, with the aim of achieving significant market share and reducing the carbon footprint of the transportation sector.



Objectives
To promote the manufacturing, adoption, and use of electric and hybrid vehicles in India.
To incentivize the production and purchase of electric vehicles through financial incentives, subsidies, and tax benefits.
To develop charging infrastructure and support services to facilitate the widespread adoption of electric vehicles.
To create awareness among consumers and stakeholders about the benefits of electric mobility and encourage the transition from conventional vehicles to electric alternatives.


Approach
The Electric Mobility Mission adopts a multi-pronged approach to promote electric mobility in India. It includes providing financial incentives and subsidies to manufacturers and consumers for the production and purchase of electric vehicles. Additionally, the mission focuses on developing charging infrastructure, research and development initiatives, skill development programs, and policy interventions to create an enabling environment for electric mobility.



Conclusion
The Electric Mobility Mission represents a significant step towards promoting sustainable transportation solutions and reducing vehicular emissions in India. Since its inception in April 2015, the mission has contributed to the growth of the electric vehicle market, the development of charging infrastructure, and the adoption of cleaner and greener transportation alternatives. However, continued efforts are needed to overcome challenges such as high upfront costs, limited charging infrastructure, and consumer awareness to realize the full potential of electric mobility in India.
